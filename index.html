Start your study abroad journey today!

Studying abroad isn't just about getting a degree; it's about experiencing life from a completely new perspective. When you choose to study overseas, you're opening doors to incredible opportunities that extend far beyond the classroom. You'll immerse yourself in a new culture, develop independence, and build a global network of connections that will benefit you throughout your career.

Why should you study abroad?

Increased Employability

According to a study in 2024, 97% of students who study abroad find employment within 12 months of graduation, compared to just 49% of those who do not. Also, students who studied abroad earn 25% higher than their peers.

Enhanced Career Skills

Over 60% of students report developing valuable career-relevant skills such as problem-solving, adaptability, and cross-cultural communication through their international education experience.

Broader Worldview

Living and studying in a foreign country intensifies your perspectives on global issues, cultures, and social norms, helping you develop a wider understanding of the world.

Real-World Work Experience

Many study abroad programs offer integrated internships, providing students with practical experience that enhances their resumes. These opportunities often lead to long-term career advantages, making graduates stand out in a competitive global job market.

Global Networking

Studying abroad connects you with professionals, mentors, and peers from around the world, expanding your professional network and opening doors to international job opportunities in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Why should I study abroad?

+

Studying abroad exposes you to a wider perspective of the world. It makes you more employable and helps you develop life skills.

Tell me the application process for studying abroad.

+

The process of studying abroad involves steps like choosing the course, destination, universities, application submission, applying for scholarships, visa application, etc.

Am I allowed to work while I study abroad?

+

Yes, a lot of countries allow part-time work. Do check your visa for the number of hours you are permitted to work weekly or fortnightly.

What is the cost of studying abroad?

+

The cost of study varies with the destination you choose; it also depends on the course and university. Please connect IDP expert to know more.
